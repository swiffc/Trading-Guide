üåô ASIAN SESSION (7:00 PM - 2:00 AM EST)

Daily Q1 - Accumulation (BTMM: Accumulation)

Characteristics:

  • Tight range consolidation (typically 20-40 pips)
  • Low volume compared to other sessions
  • Market makers building positions
  • Sets the range for London manipulation
  • EMAs typically flat or slightly trending

Strategy:

  • OBSERVE ONLY
  • Mark the Asian range high and low
  • Tight range = Expect strong breakout in London/NY
  • Extended range = Expect quieter subsequent sessions
  • NO trading - just mark levels

üåç LONDON SESSION (2:00 AM - 11:00 AM EST)

Daily Q2 - Manipulation (BTMM: Manipulation Phase)

London True Open: 1:30 AM EST

Characteristics:

  • Break of Asian range (usually around 2:00-4:00 AM)
  • "3 swipes" pattern - creates HOD or LOD (Weekly PF)
  • Spreads widen to trigger stop orders
  • Establishes High/Low of Day (HOD/LOD)
  • 30-90 min re-accumulation after initial move
  • EMA Signal: Look for 13/50 EMA cross = Level I begins
  • TDI: Sharp spike then consolidation (Shark Fin pattern)

Strategy:

  • WATCH & MARK LEVELS
  • Identify the stop hunt direction (M or W forming)
  • Mark manipulation high or low (this is your PF!)
  • Wait for 30-90 min re-accumulation
  • Prepare for NY distribution phase
  • ‚ùå DON'T trade during Level II consolidation

üåÜ NEW YORK PM SESSION (12:00 PM - 6:00 PM EST)

Daily Q4 - Reversal/Continuation

True PM Open: 1:30pm

Characteristics:

  • Volume decreases after 12pm
  • Profit-taking from morning positions
  • Price often returns toward daily open
  • Market decides next direction
  • Sets up for next day's cycle

  • Take profits from morning trades
  • Avoid new positions unless clear setup
  • Can trade pullbacks in established trend
  • Prepare for next trading day

üìä Session Interaction & Flow

Typical Daily Flow:

  1. Asian (6pm-12am): Establishes tight range
  2. London (12am-6am): Breaks range, creates HOD/LOD, stop hunt
  3. NY AM (6am-12pm): Distributes opposite to London manipulation
  4. NY PM (12pm-6pm): Continuation or reversal, profit-taking

Example Bearish Day:

  • Asian: Range 1.0850-1.0865
  • London: Pumps to 1.0895 (M pattern, stop hunt above)
  • NY: Distributes down from 1.0880 ‚Üí 1.0800 (80 pips)
  • PM: Consolidates 1.0800-1.0820, sets up next day

üîë Key Takeaways

  • Asian: Mark the range, don't trade it
  • London: Identify manipulation, mark levels
  • NY AM: Your primary trading window (9:00-10:30 AM)
  • NY PM: Manage trades, take profits
  • Best trades: Enter NY Q3 opposite to London manipulation

üìä ASIAN RANGE MONITORING & DAILY PATTERN

Using Asian Session as Daily Reference
üéØ Key Concept: The Asian session range acts as a liquidity map for London and NY sessions!

üìç What to Mark:

Asian High (AH)

  • Highest point during 7:00 PM - 2:00 AM EST
  • Acts as resistance for liquidity sweep
  • Often gets "swiped" in London session
  • Action: Mark as dashed horizontal line

Asian Low (AL)

  • Lowest point during Asian session
  • Acts as support for liquidity sweep
  • Often gets "swiped" in London session
  • Action: Mark as dashed horizontal line

Asian Midpoint (50%)

  • Halfway between AH and AL
  • Critical bounce/rejection zone
  • Price often returns to midpoint
  • Action: Mark as dotted line

Asian Range Size

  • Tight Range (< 30 pips): Expect explosive breakout
  • Normal Range (30-60 pips): Standard volatility expected
  • Wide Range (> 60 pips): Expect quieter London/NY

üéØ Daily Pattern Recognition:

Monitor the Daily Cycle for:

  1. Reversal Signals:
    • Price reaches ADR boundary
    • M or W pattern forms at HOD/LOD
    • TDI Shark Fin at extreme
    • D1 zone flip completes
  2. Continuation Signals:
    • Clean break of Asian range
    • Pullback to 50 EMA holds
    • Higher timeframe trend intact
    • No zone flip on D1
  3. Liquidity Sweeps:
    • Use Asian H/L as liquidity reference
    • Sweep = Stop hunt above/below range
    • After sweep, watch for reversal
    • 2nd leg of M/W often forms after sweep

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Rule: If Asian range is very tight (< 20 pips), expect a VIOLENT breakout in London. Be cautious with entries until direction is clear!

‚ö° Brinks Trade Timing Explained:

"Brinks Truck" - Delivering Money!

Concept: These are specific 15-minute windows where liquidity spikes occur due to institutional order flow.

üéØ Three Brinks Windows:

  • 9:45 PM EST: Asian session liquidity spike
  • 3:45 AM EST: Mid-London session spike (best for 2nd leg M/W)
  • 9:45 AM EST: NY session spike (highest volume of all three)

üîç How to Trade Brinks Windows:

  1. Watch for M or W pattern forming into the window
  2. Second leg of M/W should fall INSIDE the 15-min Brinks window
  3. Look for rejection candlestick at the apex
  4. Enter on confirmation candle after Brinks window closes
üí° Pro Tip: If a perfect M/W setup aligns with Brinks timing + key level (PDH/PDL) + EMA confluence = This is a "Masterpiece Trade"!

üö´ Stop Hunt Windows (AVOID TRADING):

Critical Rule: Hour BEFORE the 12-Candle Window

  • 7:00 PM: Stop hunt before Asian movement (9:00 PM)
  • 1:00 AM: Stop hunt before London True Open (1:30 AM)
  • 7:00 AM: Stop hunt before NY True Open (7:30 AM)

What Happens: Market makers push price to trigger stops, then reverse. This creates fake moves that trap retail traders.

Your Action: OBSERVE ONLY. Do not enter trades during these windows! Wait for the actual session to begin.

üìä 915 Rule - Multiple Timeframe Confluence:

Why 9:15 AM / 3:15 AM / 9:15 PM are special:

  • Multiple different timeframe traders are active simultaneously
  • 15-minute, 1-hour, and 4-hour chart alignments occur
  • Increased volume from various trading strategies converging
  • Creates reliable reversal or continuation setups

How to Use: Watch for setup completion around these times. If your pattern forms at 915, it has multi-timeframe backing!

üéØ BIAS DETERMINATION CHECKLIST

Determining Your Daily Bias
üéØ Before Taking ANY Trade: Answer these questions to determine if you should go long or short today.

‚úÖ Bias Checklist:

üìç 1. High/Low Status:

  • Is HOD locked in? (High of Day - no new highs for 2+ hours)
  • Is LOD locked in? (Low of Day - no new lows for 2+ hours)
  • Is HOM/HOW locked? (High of Month/Week)
  • Is LOM/LOW locked? (Low of Month/Week)

‚Üí If HOD is locked = Bearish bias. If LOD is locked = Bullish bias.

üìç 2. Session High/Low Status:

  • Is session high locked in? (Asian/London/NY session high)
  • Is session low locked in?
  • Has Asian range been swept? (Above high or below low)

‚Üí Sweep above Asian high then rejection = Bearish. Sweep below Asian low then rejection = Bullish.

üìç 3. Higher Timeframe Alignment:

  • D1 trend direction: Up or Down?
  • Any recent D1 zone flips? (Old support becomes resistance or vice versa)
  • Weekly trend direction: Up or Down?
  • Are we at ADR extreme? (90%+ of ADR met = expect reversal)

Critical: NEVER trade against a D1 zone flip! Only trade in direction of D1 trend.

üìç 4. BTMM Cycle Position:

  • What day of the week? (Monday = Day 1, Tuesday = Day 2, Wednesday = Day 3)
  • Has PF been established? (Peak Formation on Day 1 or Day 2)
  • Are we at Level III? (50/800 or 200/800 EMA cross completed)
  • Wednesday Q3 alignment? (Best day + best time = max edge)

‚Üí Trade AGAINST the PF on Day 3. If PF High on Monday/Tuesday, go SHORT on Wednesday.

üìç 5. Key Level Proximity:

  • Near PDH or PDL? (Previous Day High/Low)
  • Near PWH or PWL? (Previous Week High/Low)
  • At major EMA? (200 or 800 EMA)
  • At round number? (1.3000, 1.3100, etc.)

‚Üí Confluence at key levels = Higher probability reversal.

‚úÖ Bias Decision Matrix:

If This... Then Bias Is... Action
LOD locked + D1 uptrend + Level III confirmed BULLISH Look for W-Bottom, bullish M/W, buy setups
HOD locked + D1 downtrend + Level III confirmed BEARISH Look for M-Top, bearish M/W, sell setups

üí° Golden Rule: When in doubt, SIT IT OUT. Only trade when bias is CRYSTAL CLEAR and multiple factors align!

Quality over Quantity: 5 high-probability trades per week beats 25 mediocre ones every time.
