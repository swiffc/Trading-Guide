Push 1: London Open - Range Phase

üìç Range Phase Characteristics

Setup: They widen the swings before they make the move to hit the stops

Target: 25 to 50 pips

Purpose: The swings are widened behind the Asian Range to confuse traders

ADR Position: 1/3 ADR

Strategy:

  • DON'T take M&Ws in the middle of nowhere
  • Must be off a BT, Mayo, BB to be valid
  • Wait for Asian Range violation
  • Mark the range highs and lows
  • Prepare for Push 2

üìã Type 2 London Pattern Characteristics:

  • Time: Occurs during London session open (2-4 AM EST)
  • Structure: Break of Asian range ‚Üí Wide swing ‚Üí Retest ‚Üí Continuation
  • Volume: Increased volatility as London traders enter
  • Target: First push targets 1/3 ADR (25-50 pips typically)
  • Trap Mechanism: False breakout above/below range to trigger stops
  • Confirmation: Look for M or W pattern formation at extremes

‚úÖ How to Trade This Pattern:

Entry Rules

  1. Wait for Asian range violation
  2. Identify M or W at swing extreme
  3. Enter on break of neckline
  4. Confirm with EMA 5/13 alignment
  5. Must be off BT, Mayo, or BB level

Risk Management

  1. Stop: 10-15 pips beyond M/W extreme
  2. Target 1: Asian range opposite side
  3. Target 2: 1/3 ADR completion
  4. Move stop to BE at +15 pips
  5. Scale out: 50% at T1, 50% at T2

Push 2: Continuation - Range Phase

üìç Continuation Phase Characteristics

Setup: Price consolidates and prepares for next leg

Target: Next 1/3 ADR

Purpose: Build momentum for final push

ADR Position: 2/3 ADR cumulative

Strategy:

  • Confirm direction from Push 1
  • Look for pullback entries
  • Trade with established trend
  • Watch for EMA alignment
  • Prepare for Push 3 reversal signals
‚úÖ Best Trading Window: This is often the cleanest trend phase

Key Levels to Watch:

Half ADR Point

TDI Signal

Momentum confirmation

Push 3: Reversal Setup - Final Range Phase

üìç Reversal Phase Characteristics

Setup: Final aggressive push before reversal

Target: Remaining 1/2 ADR

Purpose: Trap final wave of retail traders

ADR Position: Full ADR met or exceeded

Reversal Signals:

  • ADR Completion: Full ADR reached (primary signal)
  • Time-Based: Approaching NY session Q3
  • Pattern-Based: M or W formation at extremes
  • EMA Divergence: Price extends too far from EMAs
  • TDI Extreme: RSI overbought/oversold
‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Warning: This is the TRAP phase - dealer makes final aggressive push to lure in late entries before reversal

Trading Push 3:

‚úÖ DO

  • Wait for ADR completion
  • Look for reversal patterns
  • Trade AGAINST the push
  • Use tight stops
  • Scale in cautiously

‚ùå DON'T

  • Chase the trend
  • Enter without ADR check
  • Ignore time-based rules
  • Use wide stops
  • Overtrade this phase
