M15 Chart Strategy - Understanding Dealer Behavior

"The dealer works on the number 3 heavily because it works on human psychology"

🧠 Core Principle: Understanding dealer behavior or mindset is key to understanding what he is thinking.

Dealer does NOT care about candles - he cares about price and contract value.

The dealer has to go against the trend to book a profit.

🔄 The 3-Push Intra-Day Pattern

1ADR (Asian Range) → PUSH 1 (1/3 ADR) → PUSH 2 (1/3 ADR) → PUSH 3 (1/2 ADR)

Push 1: London Open - Range Phase

📍 Range Phase Characteristics

Setup: They widen the swings before they make the move to hit the stops

Target: 25 to 50 pips

Purpose: The swings are widened behind the Asian Range to confuse traders

ADR Position: 1/3 ADR

Strategy:

  • DON'T take M&Ws in the middle of nowhere
  • Must be off a BT, Mayo, BB to be valid
  • Wait for Asian Range violation
  • Mark the range highs and lows
  • Prepare for Push 2
⚠️ Stop Hunt Alert: Dealer widens swings to trigger stops before real move

📊 What Happens:

Time Action Trader Response
London Open (2-3 AM EST) Price breaks Asian Range Wait & observe
First 1-2 hours Wide swings form Mark range boundaries
After 1/3 ADR met Range established Prepare for Push 2

🔍 What to Look For in Push 1:

1️⃣ Asian Range

Tight consolidation during Asia session establishes the range boundaries

2️⃣ London Break

Price violates Asian range at London open (2-3 AM EST)

3️⃣ Wide Swings

Dealer widens the range to hunt stops and confuse traders

4️⃣ Direction Established

After 1/3 ADR met, true direction becomes clear for Push 2

📋 Type 2 London Pattern Characteristics:

  • Time: Occurs during London session open (2-4 AM EST)
  • Structure: Break of Asian range → Wide swing → Retest → Continuation
  • Volume: Increased volatility as London traders enter
  • Target: First push targets 1/3 ADR (25-50 pips typically)
  • Trap Mechanism: False breakout above/below range to trigger stops
  • Confirmation: Look for M or W pattern formation at extremes

✅ How to Trade This Pattern:

Entry Rules

  1. Wait for Asian range violation
  2. Identify M or W at swing extreme
  3. Enter on break of neckline
  4. Confirm with EMA 5/13 alignment
  5. Must be off BT, Mayo, or BB level

Risk Management

  1. Stop: 10-15 pips beyond M/W extreme
  2. Target 1: Asian range opposite side
  3. Target 2: 1/3 ADR completion
  4. Move stop to BE at +15 pips
  5. Scale out: 50% at T1, 50% at T2
💡 Pro Tip: Type 2 London patterns work best when combined with daily/weekly PF levels. If London open aligns with Q2 manipulation phase on daily chart, probability increases significantly!
⚠️ Common Mistake: Don't enter on the initial Asian range break! Wait for the wide swing to form, then enter on the M/W pattern that develops at the extreme.
Push 2: Continuation - Range Phase

📍 Continuation Phase Characteristics

Setup: Price consolidates and prepares for next leg

Target: Next 1/3 ADR

Purpose: Build momentum for final push

ADR Position: 2/3 ADR cumulative

Strategy:

  • Confirm direction from Push 1
  • Look for pullback entries
  • Trade with established trend
  • Watch for EMA alignment
  • Prepare for Push 3 reversal signals
✅ Best Trading Window: This is often the cleanest trend phase

Key Levels to Watch:

EMA 5/13 Cross

Confirms trend direction

Half ADR Point

Mid-range target

TDI Signal

Momentum confirmation

Push 3: Reversal Setup - Final Range Phase

📍 Reversal Phase Characteristics

Setup: Final aggressive push before reversal

Target: Remaining 1/2 ADR

Purpose: Trap final wave of retail traders

ADR Position: Full ADR met or exceeded

Reversal Signals:

  • ADR Completion: Full ADR reached (primary signal)
  • Time-Based: Approaching NY session Q3
  • Pattern-Based: M or W formation at extremes
  • EMA Divergence: Price extends too far from EMAs
  • TDI Extreme: RSI overbought/oversold
⚠️ Critical Warning: This is the TRAP phase - dealer makes final aggressive push to lure in late entries before reversal

Trading Push 3:

✅ DO

  • Wait for ADR completion
  • Look for reversal patterns
  • Trade AGAINST the push
  • Use tight stops
  • Scale in cautiously

❌ DON'T

  • Chase the trend
  • Enter without ADR check
  • Ignore time-based rules
  • Use wide stops
  • Overtrade this phase
