M15 Chart Strategy - Understanding Dealer Behavior

"The dealer works on the number 3 heavily because it works on human psychology"

🧠 Core Principle: Dealer Psychology

Understanding dealer behavior or mindset is key to understanding what he is thinking.

Dealer does NOT care about candles - he cares about price and contract value.

The dealer has to go against the trend to book a profit.

📚 The 5 Elements of the BTMM System:

  1. The Pattern (W-V1-V2-M or M-A1-A2-W)
  2. The Pattern (confirmation of structure)
  3. The Pattern (repetition = reliability)
  4. Timing (when to enter)
  5. The Levels (where to enter)

Timing is a human condition - not all setups are equal!

Push 1 (London Open)

📍 Range Phase

Setup: They widen the swings before they make the move to hit the stops

Target: 25 to 50 pips

Purpose: The swings are widened behind the Asian Range to confuse traders

1/3 ADR

Strategy:

  • DON'T take M&Ws in the middle of nowhere
  • Must be off a BT, Mayo, BB to be valid
  • Wait for Asian Range violation
  • Mark the range highs and lows
  • Prepare for Push 2
⚠️ Stop Hunt Alert:

During the consolidation, they hit the MM stops in both directions and bring it in 20-30 pip range

2

Push 2 (NY AM Session)

📍 Range Phase

Confirmation: We are trading the structure at the LoD and HoD to validate

Tool: Use the Asian Range

1/3 ADR

Strategy:

  • Watch for Asian Range violation on Level 1
  • This is a Safety trade
  • Can't go wrong if structure violates AR
  • A straightaway bounce does NOT violate the Asian number at Level 1
  • The dealer got what he wants

⏰ Timing is Critical:

When the dealer violates the Asian Range on Level 1, it is a Safety trade - and you can't go wrong.

If the dealer presents the 2nd leg in 6pm (GMT+1), that is a no trade condition. Why? Because the dealer's job is to go into consolidation.

3

Push 3 (NY PM Session)

📍 Range Phase

Final Move: The highest probability setup

Psychology: Works on human psychology - dealer behavior on number 3

1/2 ADR

Strategy:

  • He may run sometimes, but your job is to take the highest probability setups
  • Timing is just as important as any other stuff
  • If the dealer presents the 2nd leg in 6pm (GMT+1), that is a no trade condition
  • Why? Because the dealer's job is to go into consolidation (Asia)
  • Hit the stops one more time in Late Asia/London. He may run sometimes
⛔ DO NOT HOLD TRADE OVER THE WEEKEND EVER

You cannot control what happens in this world.

You only can control what happens in your world and the way you control that is to be flat on Friday.

📊 The Level System

Level 1

First Asian Range Touch

  • Safety trade
  • Can't go wrong
  • Violation = Entry signal

Level 2

Second Touch

  • Confirmation building
  • Watch dealer behavior
  • Pattern developing

Level 3

Third Touch - MAXIMUM PROBABILITY

  • Highest probability setup
  • Dealer psychology at work
  • Take the trade with confidence
Note: A level or push must move higher/lower than a previous move, or it is not a level.

⚠️ Stop Hunt Behavior:

Most of the time, the stop will be triggered before the next level is started.

When the previous day's or another day's high/low is violated and the dealer closes back below or back above the high (notice the blue tracer), that is a sure sign that the dealer will correct off of that level.

🤖 Dealer vs 👤 Human Conditions

🤖 Dealer Conditions:

  • Not the same as human conditions
  • MM may not move the market when you want
  • Works on price and contract value
  • Must book profit against trend
  • Operates on number 3 psychology

👤 Human Conditions:

  • Emotional trading decisions
  • Chase entries (FOMO)
  • Move stops when losing
  • Get trapped by dealer moves
  • Trade in the middle of nowhere

"Forcing one's beliefs on the market can affect one's patience. Understanding what the big picture and dealer psychology gives a deeper understanding of what is going on leading to patience and calmness"
