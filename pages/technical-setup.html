These technical indicators reveal when market makers are present (Level I/III) or absent (Level II). Without these tools, you're trading blind.

üìà 2. TDI (TRADERS DYNAMIC INDEX)

Your Momentum Confirmation Tool

What is TDI?

TDI combines RSI (momentum) with moving averages to show:

  • Momentum direction (bullish or bearish)
  • Momentum strength (strong or weak)
  • Reversal signals (when momentum shifts)
  • Entry confirmation (with EMA crosses)

TDI Components:

Component Setting Color Purpose
RSI 13 or 21 period Yellow line Momentum oscillator
Fast MA (Signal) 2-period SMA üü¢ Green line Fast signal line
Slow MA (Baseline) 7-period SMA üî¥ Red line Slow signal line
Market Base Line 50 level Grey (middle) Bullish/bearish divide
Volatility Bands 32, 50, 68 Grey dotted Extreme zones

How to Read TDI:

Bullish Signals:

  • üü¢ Green line ABOVE Red line = Bullish momentum
  • üìà RSI above Market Base Line (50) = Bullish bias
  • üîµ RSI above 68 = Overbought (potential reversal)
  • ‚úÖ Green crosses above Red = Entry signal

Bearish Signals:

  • üî¥ Red line ABOVE Green line = Bearish momentum
  • üìâ RSI below Market Base Line (50) = Bearish bias
  • üî¥ RSI below 32 = Oversold (potential reversal)
  • ‚úÖ Red crosses above Green = Entry signal

  • Momentum extreme reached
  • Sharp spike in RSI then reversal

Trading it: When RSI crosses back through middle line (50 MBL) after spike = ENTRY signal!

üìç 4. PIVOT POINTS

Market Maker Target Levels

What are Pivots?

Pivot points are calculated support/resistance levels based on previous day's high, low, and close. Market makers LOVE to target these levels.

Pivot Levels:

Level Name Function
R3 Resistance 3 Extreme resistance (rarely reached)
R2 Resistance 2 Strong resistance, potential target
R1 Resistance 1 First resistance, common target
PP Pivot Point Central pivot - key level!
S1 Support 1 First support, common target
S2 Support 2 Strong support, potential target
S3 Support 3 Extreme support (rarely reached)

How to Use Pivots:

‚úÖ DO:

  • Use as take profit targets
  • Watch for reversals at pivots
  • Combine with M/W patterns
  • Stop losses beyond pivots

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Trade pivots alone (need confluence)
  • Ignore price action at pivots
  • Assume all pivots will hold
  • Place stops exactly at pivots

üìè 5. ADR (AVERAGE DAILY RANGE)

Know When to Stop Trading

What is ADR?

Average Daily Range measures the typical high-to-low movement for a currency pair. Once ADR is met, probability of continued move DROPS significantly.

Common ADR Values:

Pair Typical ADR Note
EUR/USD 70-90 pips Most stable
GBP/USD 100-120 pips More volatile
USD/JPY 60-80 pips Consistent range
EUR/JPY 100-140 pips High volatility

How to Use ADR:

  • ‚úÖ Set realistic targets - Don't expect 150 pips if ADR is 80
  • ‚úÖ Reduce trading - After ADR met, probability drops
  • ‚úÖ Watch for reversals - At ADR limits, expect reversal
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è Stop new entries - When ADR 90%+ met, avoid new trades

üìç 5. KEY LEVEL TRACKING

Critical Levels to Mark Daily
üéØ Daily Ritual: Mark these levels EVERY morning before trading. They act as magnets for price and reversal zones!

üìÖ Previous Period Levels:

Previous Day (PDH/PDL)

  • PDH: Previous Day High
  • PDL: Previous Day Low
  • Use: Primary intraday support/resistance
  • Action: Mark as horizontal lines

Previous Week (PWH/PWL)

  • PWH: Previous Week High
  • PWL: Previous Week Low
  • Use: Major swing levels
  • Action: Mark as dashed lines

Previous Month (PMH/PML)

  • PMH: Previous Month High
  • PML: Previous Month Low
  • Use: Strong institutional levels
  • Action: Mark with thick lines

Yearly Extremes (PYH/PYL)

  • PYH: Previous Year High
  • PYL: Previous Year Low
  • Use: Major reversal zones
  • Action: Mark as colored zones

üìä Session-Specific Levels:

Level Abbrev. Description Use Case

üìè ADR Boundaries:

5-Day Average Daily Range (ADR):

  • Calculate: Average of last 5 days' high-low ranges
  • Mark: ADR High and ADR Low from current day's open
  • Use: Profit targets and reversal expectations
  • Rule: When ADR 90%+ met, expect reversal or consolidation

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Rule - D1 Zone Flips:

üö´ NEVER Trade Against D1 Zone Flips!

When Daily (D1) timeframe shows a zone flip (support becomes resistance or vice versa), DO NOT trade against the higher timeframe trend.

  • Zone Flip Up: Old resistance becomes new support = Bullish bias
  • Zone Flip Down: Old support becomes new resistance = Bearish bias
  • Action: Only take trades IN THE DIRECTION of the D1 flip

‚úÖ Daily Marking Checklist:

  • Mark Weekly PSR Zone (first 8 H1 candles - Sunday 5PM to Monday 1AM EST)
  • Mark PDH and PDL (previous day high/low)
  • Mark PWH and PWL (previous week high/low)
  • Mark PMH and PML (previous month high/low)
  • Mark PYH and PYL (previous year high/low)
  • Calculate and mark 5-day ADR boundaries
  • Mark Asian session high and low
  • Check D1 for recent zone flips
  • Update HOD/LOD as day progresses
  • Note session highs/lows (PSH/PSL)
üìä PSR Zone: The first 8 hourly candles (Sunday 5PM-Monday 1AM EST) create the weekly Psychological Support/Resistance zone. Draw a box around the high/low of these candles - price will respect this zone all week! See BTMM Cycle page for complete PSR details.

üí° Pro Tip: These levels work because institutional traders use them. When you mark them, you're seeing what the "smart money" sees!

Confluence is King: Best setups occur when M/W patterns form AT these key levels + EMA confluence + correct session timing!

üìè ADR-BASED SETUPS

AR 00 / AR 50 - Asian Range Level Setups

Concept:

AR = Asian Range: The high-to-low range established during Asian session (5:00 PM - 2:00 AM EST)

AR 00 (Asian Range 00): The TOP (Asian High) and BOTTOM (Asian Low) of the Asian session range

AR 50 (Asian Range 50): The MIDPOINT (50%) of the Asian session range

Why They Work: Asian range boundaries act as key liquidity zones. London/NY sessions often sweep AR 00 (high/low) then reverse, or bounce off AR 50 (midpoint) for continuation.

Setup Process:

Identify:

  1. Calculate today's ADR high and low
  2. Mark major round numbers (00 levels)
  3. Mark half levels (50 levels)
  4. Watch for price approach

Entry Types:

Bounce Play:

  • Price approaches AR level
  • Rejection candle forms
  • Enter in direction of rejection
  • Stop beyond level + 20 pips

Break & Retest:

  • Price breaks through AR level
  • Pulls back to retest
  • Rejection on retest
  • Enter in break direction

Best Combined With:

  • ‚úÖ M/W patterns at these levels (double confluence!)
  • ‚úÖ Level 2 or 3 of 3-Day Cycle
  • ‚úÖ Session opens (London 1:30 AM, NY 7:30 AM EST)
  • ‚úÖ Major EMA confluence (50, 200, 800)

Risk Management:

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: AR levels act as magnets - don't fight the pull to these levels!

Once ADR met: Expect reversal or consolidation. Avoid new entries when ADR 90%+ complete.
